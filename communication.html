Communication

Teaching touch in Bandung

As a part of the intensive Indonesian language course I attended while in the Army, the students and teachers spent two weeks in Indonesia doing various language exercises everyday. The students had to pick from a range of different communication assignments to showcase our ability to use the Indonesian language, and my assignment was to deliver a Physical Training (PT) class to a group of Year 12 students at a school in Bandung, Indonesia. Not only did this showcase my ability to communicate to a group, but to a group of people from a different culture and using a different language.

When I was first given the task of delivering a PT class to students at a school, I had to assess what sort of subject I could effectively communicate to the students without getting bogged down with too much intricate terminology. I also wanted to make sure I could get my ideas across and ensure the students wouldn't get bored and would enjoy the class. I decided to teach them how to play Touch Footy, (modified rugby league rules) as I was an avid player at the time and this made the task a lot easier as I had a passion for this sport. I practiced delivering my lesson to colleagues and Indonesian counterparts before my final assignment. The assignment was recorded and assessed.

The PT class was a success, I was able to effectively communicate the basic concepts behind playing Touch Footy and the whole class was able to play and they had a great time! The PT teacher for the school even got in and played the game and said that he would like to implement Touch Footy into the curricular and asked for a copy of the rules. As a further result, on completion of this course I was given the following feedback by the head lecturer of the language course when I was awarded a Diploma of Vocational Indonesian Language.

The main thing I learnt from this experience was that if you want to communicate a message effectively, doesn't matter what language you speak, if you make it fun then it is easier to get your message across. In this case, it was creating an engaging sporting environment but in another case it may mean injecting some humour into what you say.
