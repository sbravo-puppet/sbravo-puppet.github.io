Teamwork

Completing the Kapooka Challenge

When I first joined the Army in 2007 I wasn't sure what I was in for, but you do quickly find out what it's all about when you get to Kapooka, just outside of Wagga Wagga in NSW. During the recruit training you are taught the basic core skills of a soldier and that includes the Army's core values which are Courage, Initiative, Respect and Teamwork. During the final phase of recruit training, as a platoon (team of 30 - 40) we had to work together to negotiate an obstacle course, bayonet assault course and conduct a coordinated attack on a notional enemy. All of which requires people to follow orders and carry out their roles and look after their mates. Teamwork is of the utmost importance.

This was one of the first times I had really been pushed and where I was required to use the skills I had learnt in the previous 10 weeks. Myself and another person were selected to be the Lead Scouts of the platoon, which required us to always be at the far front while the platoon was moving. Our jobs included relaying information back to the section commander and to the rest of the team. During the coordinated attack, we were always required to stretcher carry some of our notionally wounded team mates. This required coordinated teamwork so that we weren't always carrying the same sides of the stretcher.

Got through the 'Kapooka Challenge' and graduated from 1 Recruit Training Battalion (1RTB) and went from being 'Recruit' to 'Private'. I learnt so much from my time at Kapooka about teamwork. People from all walks of life from all over Australia are forced to work together as a close-knit team, and have to be able to rely on them to not only do multiple-person jobs, but also for motivation and emotional support. We were always told to spur our mates on, to make sure we use our voices, loud and clear, so that everyone knew we were part of the team!

I learnt a lot about myself from this experience; that I was able to push myself past mental and physical boundaries I had not yet tested and that many hands make light work! Understanding that a team works well when everyone gets in and helps but also when there is a common goal and everyone supports everyone else. This is the type of mental attitude I intend on bringing to future endeavours.
